Insurance Dictionary

Coverage Terms

Coverage

Coverage is the protection and benefits provided in an insurance contract. Insurance coverage helps consumers recover financially from unexpected events. A trusted insurance agent can help you select the best coverage to suit your needs.

Actual Cash Value

Actual cash value is the amount you’ll be reimbursed for what the items or damage cost to repair or replace, minus the depreciation. Actual cash value is likely to be different than the replacement cost value.

Loss and Peril

In insurance, "loss" signifies the financial damage resulting from an event like an accident or theft, while "peril" refers to the specific cause of that loss, such as a fire or flood. Insurance policies protect against different perils, covering the resulting losses and aiding policyholders in recovering from unexpected setbacks

Real World Examples

Greg

Greg was driving and hit a deer. Greg’s insurance plan paid for his car to be repaired due to his Coverage plan.

April

April’s house burnt down and she lost everything. After inspection she received the Actual Cash Value of her lost items minus depreciation.

Bill

A tornado ripped through Bill’s town totaling his house. He filed a claim to his insurance company in which his house was filed as a loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

    How is it determined who is at fault?

  • At fault, or liability, will be determined based on the accident and resulting damage. It is possible that both victims are found responsible.

    • What happens after I file a claim?

  • After filing a claim, make sure to keep any affected items and document any of damage to the item. This is so your insurance adjuster can accurately estimate your damages.

    • Do you have to fill out an accident report form?

  • All drivers involved in an accident are required to fill out the accident report form to ensure the accident is well documented
