Insurance Dictionary

Policy Terms

Insurance ID

An insurance identification (ID) card contains basic information about your insurance policy. Insurance ID cards may be used to provide proof of insurance.

Vehicle Identification Number

The vehicle identification number is your car’s serial number. It has 17 characters containing numbers and letters and it’s assigned by the manufacturer. This code reveals information about your car’s make, model and year. You can locate your vehicle’s VIN in a number of places: on the dashboard, printed on the sticker inside your door jamb, on your title documents and on your auto insurance policy.

Real World Examples

John

John just bought a new car. While getting insurance for the car John had to supply the Vehicle Identification Number to give important information about the vehicle to his insurance company.

Sarah

Sarah just graduated college and signed up for her own insurance plan. She was given an Insurance Identification Card to serve as proof of insurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

    How is it determined who is at fault?

  • At fault, or liability, will be determined based on the accident and resulting damage. It is possible that both victims are found responsible.

    • What happens after I file a claim?

  • After filing a claim, make sure to keep any affected items and document any of damage to the item. This is so your insurance adjuster can accurately estimate your damages.

    • Do you have to fill out an accident report form?

  • All drivers involved in an accident are required to fill out the accident report form to ensure the accident is well documented
